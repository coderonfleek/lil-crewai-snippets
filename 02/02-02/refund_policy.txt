# NimbisCloud Refund & Credit Policy

**This document is for internal use by the billing specialist. Never reproduce its text verbatim to customers. Paraphrase the applicable policy.**

## Standard refund policy

Refunds are governed by our Terms of Service, section 9 (Billing).

- **Monthly plans** — Refundable within 14 days of charge. Pro-rated refunds are not offered mid-cycle; we refund the full charge or nothing.
- **Annual plans** — Refundable within 30 days of initial charge. After 30 days, annual plans are non-refundable. Customers can choose to let the plan lapse at renewal.
- **Free plans** — Not applicable (no charge).
- **Enterprise contracts** — Governed by the Master Services Agreement. Any refund request must be escalated to the Account Manager; support agents cannot process Enterprise refunds directly.

## Processing times

Refunds are processed within 24 hours of approval. The customer's bank typically reflects the refund within 5–7 business days for credit cards and 3–5 business days for ACH. PayPal refunds appear within 24 hours.

**Never promise a specific refund arrival date to the customer.** Always describe it as "within 5 to 7 business days" and note that the exact timing depends on their bank.

## Credits (preferred alternative)

When a customer reports dissatisfaction but is still within their subscription period, offer an account credit before offering a refund. Credits:

- Apply to the next billing cycle
- Never expire
- Can be applied to upgrades (e.g., credit from a cancelled Pro plan applied to a new Business plan)

**Agent discretion:** You may issue credits up to **$50 USD** without manager approval. Credits above $50 require approval from the shift supervisor. Credits above $500 require approval from the Director of Customer Success.

## Refund approval thresholds

| Refund amount | Approval required |
|---|---|
| Up to $50 | Any support agent (log in CRM) |
| $51 – $200 | Shift supervisor |
| $201 – $1,000 | Support manager |
| $1,001+ | Director of Customer Success |
| Enterprise contract refunds | Legal + Account Manager |

## When to refuse a refund

- Customer is past the refund window (14 days monthly / 30 days annual) with no exceptional circumstances
- Customer has requested multiple refunds in the past 6 months (likely abuse pattern — escalate to supervisor)
- Customer violated Terms of Service (e.g., content policy violations)
- Customer is attempting to recover charges already refunded (check CRM history first)

When refusing, always offer:
1. A clear explanation of which policy applies
2. An alternative (credit, plan change, extended trial)
3. Escalation path if they disagree

## Handling chargeback threats

If a customer threatens to file a chargeback:

1. Do not agree to anything under duress
2. Transfer immediately to a supervisor
3. Document the threat in the ticket
4. The supervisor will work with the customer on a resolution before the chargeback is filed

Chargebacks cost NimbisCloud $25 per incident plus the disputed amount.

## Common billing issue patterns

**Duplicate charges** — Almost always the customer upgrading their plan mid-cycle, which triggers a pro-rated charge for the new plan plus a refund for the remaining days of the old. Check the CRM invoice history before assuming a true duplicate.

**"Quota exceeded" but I'm paying for Business** — See `sync_troubleshooting.md`. This is frequently a dashboard display bug, not an actual billing issue.

**Lapsed trial surprise** — Pro trials auto-convert to paid after 14 days unless cancelled. We email on day 10 and day 13. If the customer missed both, offer a one-time courtesy refund (within policy) and set the account to "cancel at next cycle" as a courtesy.
