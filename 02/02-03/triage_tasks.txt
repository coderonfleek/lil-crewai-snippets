classify_ticket:
  description: >
    Read the following support ticket and produce a structured triage decision.

    --- TICKET ---
    Subject: {subject}
    Description: {description}

    --- CUSTOMER ---
    Email: {customer_email}
    Name: {customer_name}
    Plan tier: {tier}
    ---

    Steps:
    1. Determine the issue CATEGORY from: billing, technical, account, api, general.
    2. Determine the PRIORITY from: urgent, high, normal, low.
       Factor in the plan tier: Business/Enterprise customers get at least `high`
       unless the issue is trivial. Enterprise customers with production outages
       get `urgent`.
    3. Produce a confidence score [0.0–1.0] reflecting how sure you are. If the
       ticket is ambiguous or could plausibly be two categories, score below 0.5.
    4. Set `needs_human = true` if the ticket mentions legal threats, chargebacks,
       data loss on an Enterprise account, harmful content, or anything
       else a human should review before the system responds.
    5. Suggest 2-5 tags to apply to the Zendesk ticket.
    6. Write a one-sentence `reasoning` explanation.

    Ground your classification in the knowledge sources. If the ticket mentions
    "quota exceeded" on Business tier for example, you should recognize that
    as a documented known-issue pattern.
  expected_output: >
    A TriageDecision object with category, priority, confidence, reasoning,
    suggested_tags, and needs_human populated.
  agent: triage_specialist
